Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2065288578364575892)

Here are verses from rAmAyaNa, where all the rakShas-es are described by similies using the gods --  we see vAlmIki deploying the famous device of the reverse simile seen in the itihAsa-purANa:

sa rarAja rathe tasmin rAjasUnur mahAbalaH |
vR^ito nairR^ita-shArdUlair vajra-pANir ivAmaraiH ||
That exceedingly powerful royal prince shone in his chariot, surrounded by the tigers among the nairR^ita-s, just like the wielder of the vajra surrounded by the immortal gods. (trishiras)

hayam uchchaiHshravaH prakhyaM shvetaM kanaka-bhUShaNam |
manojavaM mahAkAyam Aruroha narAntakaH ||
gR^ihItvA prAsam ulkAbhaM virarAja narAntakaH |
shaktim AdAya tejasvI guhaH shatruShv ivAhave ||

narAntaka mounted a colossal, brilliant white horse adorned with golden ornaments, which resembled uchchaiHshravas itself, moving as fast as the mind (_mano-javam_).
Grasping his spear, which flashed like a blazing meteor, the radiant narAntaka shone magnificently, like the fiery guha wielding his spear against his enemies in battle.

devAntakaH samAdAya parighaM vajrabhUShaNam |
parigR^ihya giriM dorbhyAM vapur viShNor viDambayan ||

devAntaka, lifting up a spiked mace (parigha) studded with adamants, [appeared as though] he were carrying a whole mountain with his two, [mimicking] the [colossal] form of viShNu.

mahApArshvo mahAtejA gadAm AdAya vIryavAn |
virarAja gadA-pANiH kubera iva saMyuge ||
The highly radiant, manly mahApArshva, wielding his mace, stood resplendent like kubera on the field of combat.
